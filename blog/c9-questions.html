Due to my practice in philosophy, I have a lot of experience asking good questions.

Some of the premier tips for asking good questions are the following:

  • Give a specific, simple example of what you're asking about. No frills. Contextualize the problem being talked about.

    When it comes to programming questions, this often means providing a simplified version of the problem. It's great practice to replace unnecessary details with toy phrases like foo and bar, and to include this simplified version of the problem in a question on StackOverflow.
  • Don't actualy ask a question--make a statement instead. For example, instead of it may be tempting to ask little more than , "how would I use Bootstrap to vertically align text in a div?" If you're expressing a point of view, state it as a theory. "In this situation, it seems to me like I should do such-and-such. Does that seem on track?"
  • The best questions are the ones that get asked. The best ones that get asked penetrate to general principles but apply them sraightforwardly to a case.
