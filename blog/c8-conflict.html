Insights from a former whiner

2015.6.19

This week I thought I'd try an audio file as a way of divulging my thoughts on unsolicited feedback.



The lessons I sum up at the end:

  1. Use a 5:1 ratio of positive-to-negative comments.

    2. Harvard Business Review has a piece about this ratio for groups. Regarding the 5:1 ratio's usefulness for marriages, Psychology Today is one of many internet sites that attributes research to the famous psychologist John Gottman, but I've yet to uncover any original study on the issue.

  2. Reserve feedback for proper forums, wherever possible.
  3. Reserve judgment and be tolerant of other ways of doing things.
  4. Try not to be a perfectionist, especially about other people's stuff.
  5. If you give unsolicited feedback, make sure conditions are truly right.

Conditions for unsolicited feedback (all of these should probably obtain before proceeding):

  1. Make sure there's no reserved forum for negative feedback to be voiced.
  2. Word things constructively: lightly, kindly; not annoyingly, offensively, or devastatingly.
  3. Present it from the perspective of your own feelings and perceptions rather than as an objective fact.
  4. Don’t offer thorough diagnoses of someone else’s problem; and don’t offer treatment plans or prognoses.
